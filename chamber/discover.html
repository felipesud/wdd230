History

The name of the municipality originates from the Tupi language and means clearing or "passage in the forest", from ka'a, "forest" and asapaba, "passage". Probably, the name was given due to the large natural crevice that exists in the Serra do Mar in this region and which is responsible for the dense fogs coming from the Atlantic Ocean in the winter period.

Caçapava emerged through two different nuclei. The oldest nucleus, which today is the neighborhood of Vila Velha de Caçapava, was a village that grew up around the Nossa Senhora d'Ajuda chapel, built in 1705 on the land of a farm belonging to Jorge Dias Velho and landing place of the path. Real that connected the municipalities of São Paulo and Taubaté.

Caçapava Velha (as the village is known today) was elevated to parish status on March 18, 1813 with the name Cassapaba parish. Today's Caçapava appeared in 1842, the year in which a chapel dedicated to Saint John the Baptist was built. The village was founded by Captain João Ramos da Silva and became the parish seat. On May 3, 1850, it was elevated to the category of village, becoming a municipality on April 14, 1855.

After coffee at the end of the 19th century, a period of economic stagnation followed. The recovery only took place in the mid-20th century with the cultivation of rice and the introduction of dairy farming, and it accelerated in the 1970s with the expansion of industrial activities in the municipality.
